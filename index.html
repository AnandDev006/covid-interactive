Coronavirus

Coronavirus Life Cycle

Coronavirus is a family of viruses that causes respiratory illness, such as the common cold and Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome-2 (SARS CoV-2), the virus that causes COVID-19.

These viruses have projections on their surface that have led to their name corona meaning crown. They are a type of RNA, enveloped virus that has a complex capsid shape.

The viral projections or 'spikes' on SARS CoV-2 enters cells by attaching its spike proteins to the receptor on the enzyme ACE2 that can be found on various cells throughout the body.

Once the spike attaches to the receptors, the virus envelope fuses with the cell membrane of the host cell and RNA from the virus is released into the host cell.

The virus uses the machinery of a host cell to produce new copies of the virus.

The replicated virus particles are released from the host cell, able to infect other cells of the host.

Immune System

INNATE IMMUNE SYSTEM

(FAST RESPONSE)

ADAPTIVE IMMUNE SYSTEM

(SLOW RESPONSE)

The immune system protects the body against disease or other potentially damaging foreign bodies

The innate immune system provides a quick and non-specific response to to viruses

It includes mechanical barriers (skin and mucous membranes) that normally prevent viruses form entering the body

It also includes responses to viruses that may overcome these barriers. For example, certain leukocytes (white blood cells) use phagocytosis to engulf and destroy viruses

The adaptive immune system is activated if viruses successfully enter the body and overcome the general defenses of the innate immune system. B and T cell activation occurs as part of the immune response

Memory B and T cells may remain in the body to protect the body from re-infection. This is known as immunity.

How Coronavirus Spreads

The virus is thought to spread through close person-to-person contact (e.g. between people within about 6 feet of each other) from droplets of a cough, sneeze, and talking

People may become infected after the virus gets transferred to the mouth or nose, or inhaled into the lungs. There is evidence that the virus may spread by people who do not have symptoms (asymptomatic and pre-symptomatic).

Why does soap work ?

Soap molecules have two different ends. The head of the soap molecule is polar and the tail end is nonpolar

Soap molecules have two different ends. The head of the soap molecule is polar and the tail end is nonpolar. Having polar and nonpolar parts allows soap to interact with polar molecules such as water and nonpolar materials such as lipids in membranes. The enveloped coronavirus contains lipids, making the virus more vulnerable to inactivation by soap.

Prevention & Treatment

Vaccines can be used to prevent viral infections. A vaccine is a mixture of antigenic material and other immune stimulants that will produce immunity to a certain pathogen or disease.

The material in the vaccine can either be weakened forms of a living pathogen or virus, dead pathogens (or inactivated viruses), purified material such as viral proteins, or genetically engineered pieces of a pathogen. Antiviral drugs, medications used specifically for treating the symptoms of viral infections, can also be used.
