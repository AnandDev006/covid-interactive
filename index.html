Coronavirus Life Cycle

Coronavirus is a family of viruses that causes respiratory illness, such as the common cold and Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome-2 (SARS CoV-2), the virus that causes COVID-19.

These viruses have projections on their surface that have led to their name corono meaning crown. They are a type of RNA, enveloped virus that has a complex capsid shape.

The viral projections or 'spikes' on SARS CoV-2 enters cells by attaching its spike proteins to the receptor on the enzyme ACE2 that can be found on various cells throughout the body.

Once the spike attaches to the receptors, the virus envelope fuses with the cell membrane of the host cell and RNA from the virus is released into the host cell.

The virus uses the machinery of a host cell to produce new copies of the virus.

The replicated virus particles are released from the host cell, able to infect other cells of the host.
